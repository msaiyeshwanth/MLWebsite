Chart-1:

Source:

The relationship between voting for the Democrats and age in US presidential elections from 1972 to 2020

Context:

  1. Why: why was this chart created (by the chart creators)?

    2. To show the influence of young voters (under 25) on presidential election results, particularly in the context of a poll boost for Joe Biden.

  2. Who: who was this chart created for?

    3. The target audience are public, political analysts, journalists, and policymakers interested in understanding the dynamics of US elections.

  3. What: what did the chart creators want the target audience to know/do?

    4. About trends and patterns that show the increasing significance of young voters (under 25) in shaping election results over time.

  4. How: what data/data source did the chart creators use (if you know)?

    5. The data is sourced from the American National Election Study (ANES).

After:

Improvements Made to the Chart:

  1. Removed the chart border
  2. Removed the grid lines

Chart-2:

Source:

On gun violence, the United States is an outlier

Context:

  1. Why: why was this chart created (by the chart creators)?

    2. To understand percentage of childhood deaths caused by firearms among high-income countries with populations over 10 million in 2021.

  2. Who: who was this chart created for?

    3. The chart was likely created for policymakers, public health officials, researchers, and the public interested in understanding and addressing firearm violence.

  3. What: what did the chart creators want the target audience to know/do?

    4. How much gun violence accounts to childhood deaths in different high-income countries with populations over 10 million in 2021. creators aimed to emphasize the importance of addressing gun violence as a public health issue.

  4. How: what data/data source did the chart creators use (if you know)?

    5. The data source is Scott Glenn from the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME), who is listed as a data specialist in the article. The specific data points regarding firearm homicide rates in various countries around the world were likely obtained from public health databases, governmental reports, and other relevant sources.

Before:

After:

Improvements Made to the Chart:

  1. Removed the chart border
  2. Reduced the cluttering by combining countries with significantly less values into others
  3. Removed the X axis and directly annotated values.
  4. Added the X axis label at the top
  5. Changed the title to boost the signal
  6. Leveraged the Gestalt principle of similarity and applied the same color to the title to match with the outlier.
